The Maker

Katerina
Papaphilotheou

Katerina is a graphic designer who found her true passion in the ancient craft of letterpress printing. She set up her studio in Limassol in 2012, drawn by the tactile beauty of type pressed deep into luxurious paper — a sensation no digital process can replicate.

Every piece that leaves the studio carries the mark of the press: a slight indent, an honest impression, a reminder that something real was made here.

The Craft

Pressed
by Hand

Letterpress is one of the oldest forms of printing — and still one of the most beautiful. Each piece is fed through the press by hand, one sheet at a time, leaving a deep impression in the paper that you can feel with your fingertips.

Katerina works closely with each client from first sketch to final print, on paper that is chosen as carefully as the design itself.

The Press — No. 1

The
Nebiolo

Found by Isidoros next door and rescued from obscurity, this early 20th–century Italian press is now the studio’s workhorse. Over a century old and still printing flawlessly.

Origin: Italy  ·  Era: Early 1900s

The Press — No. 2

Harry

A Golding Jobber made near Boston in the late 19th century, Harry travelled via South Africa before arriving in Cyprus in 1932. Named with affection, he brings character and history to every impression.

Origin: Boston  ·  Era: Late 1800s  ·  Arrived Cyprus: 1932
